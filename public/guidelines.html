Community Guidelines

Last updated: July 5, 2026  ·  All users must follow these guidelines.

Campus Hands is built on trust — between students looking to earn, and community members who need help. These guidelines exist to keep everyone safe, respected, and treated fairly. Violations may result in account suspension or permanent removal from the platform.

These guidelines apply to everyone — task posters, student workers, and anyone who interacts with the Platform.

For Everyone

🤝 Be Honest

  • Provide accurate and truthful information at all times — about yourself, your tasks, and your experience.
  • Do not misrepresent your age, identity, skills, or qualifications.
  • Do not create fake accounts or impersonate another person.
  • Do not submit fraudulent reviews, ratings, or applications.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Treat Everyone With Respect

  • No harassment, threats, or intimidation of any kind.
  • No discrimination based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, national origin, or any other protected characteristic.
  • No sexual harassment or inappropriate conduct of any kind — especially toward minors.
  • No hate speech, slurs, or content that demeans others.
  • Communicate professionally and courteously in all platform interactions.

💳 Use Payments Honestly

  • All payments must go through the Campus Hands platform. Do not arrange, accept, or encourage off-platform payments (cash, Venmo, Zelle, etc.) for work originating on Campus Hands.
  • Do not attempt to manipulate the payment or escrow system.
  • Posters must release payment promptly once work is satisfactorily completed.
  • Students must not request payment before the agreed work is done.

🔒 Protect Privacy

  • Do not share another user's personal information (name, address, phone number, email) outside the Platform.
  • Do not screenshot, record, or distribute any private communications.
  • Task addresses shared with accepted students must be used only for the purpose of completing the task.

For Task Posters

📋 Post Accurate Tasks

  • Describe the work accurately and completely. Do not mislead students about the nature, duration, or difficulty of a task.
  • Disclose all relevant job site conditions: pets, stairs, heavy lifting, outdoor work, etc.
  • Set a fair and reasonable pay rate for the work involved.
  • Do not bait-and-switch — if the task changes significantly after a student is hired, negotiate and agree before proceeding.

👶 Hiring Minors — Your Responsibility

  • You are engaging a person who may be between 16 and 24 years old. You are solely responsible for providing a safe work environment.
  • Do not ask students to perform any task that is dangerous, illegal, or that they are not legally permitted to do under applicable child labor laws.
  • Students must not work in isolated or unsafe conditions. Ensure someone else is present or contactable.
  • Students should never be alone in a home or enclosed space with a single adult unless the task specifically and unavoidably requires it (e.g., in-home pet care).
  • For babysitting jobs: provide emergency contacts, home rules, and any relevant medical information about the children in your care.
  • Do not ask students to work past 9:00 PM, or at times restricted by your state's child labor laws.

🚫 Prohibited Tasks — Do Not Post

  • Electrical work, wiring, or circuit panel work
  • Plumbing, pipe fitting, or drain work
  • Roofing, gutter work, or work at heights above a standard ladder
  • Chainsaw use or tree climbing
  • Work involving firearms, weapons, or ammunition
  • Handling of hazardous chemicals, pesticides, or asbestos
  • Medical care, nursing, or administration of any medication
  • Alcohol service or bartending
  • Passenger transportation (driving people)
  • Overnight childcare or supervision
  • Anything illegal under federal, state, or local law
  • Any task requiring a professional license (contractor, cosmetologist, lawyer, financial advisor, etc.)

For Student Workers

⏰ Be Reliable

  • If you apply to a task and are accepted, show up. Repeated no-shows will result in account suspension.
  • Communicate promptly with the poster — let them know if you're running late or need to reschedule.
  • Complete the work you agreed to, to the standard described in the task.
  • If you're unable to complete a task, notify the poster as soon as possible.

🛡️ Know Your Rights and Limits

  • You have the right to refuse any task or instruction that feels unsafe, illegal, or inappropriate. Leave immediately and report the situation to Campus Hands at safety@campushands.app.
  • You must not work past 9:00 PM (or earlier per your state's child labor laws) regardless of what a poster requests.
  • You must not perform any work listed in the prohibited tasks section, even if a poster asks you to.
  • Trust your instincts. If something feels wrong, leave and contact a trusted adult or call 911.

💼 Represent Yourself Professionally

  • Be respectful and professional in all communications with posters.
  • Do not accept off-platform payment offers.
  • Do not solicit tips, additional payments, or personal contact information beyond what is needed for the task.
  • Do not take photos of a poster's home or belongings without explicit permission.

Age-Restricted Categories

The following job categories require workers to be 18 years of age or older. The platform enforces this automatically, but students are also expected to self-report honestly:

  • Babysitting & Childcare
  • Heavy Moving & Hauling
  • Event Staffing
  • Furniture Assembly

Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness a violation of these guidelines, please report it:

  • Safety emergency: Call 911 immediately.
  • Safety concern or incident: safety@campushands.app
  • Abuse, harassment, or policy violation: support@campushands.app
  • Legal matter: legal@campushands.app

We take all reports seriously. Accounts found to be in violation of these guidelines may be suspended immediately pending investigation, and may be permanently removed from the platform. Serious violations may be referred to law enforcement.

Consequences of Violations

  • First offense (minor): Warning and required acknowledgment of guidelines.
  • Repeated minor violations: Temporary account suspension.
  • Serious violation (harassment, fraud, safety incident): Immediate permanent ban.
  • Involving a minor in an unsafe situation: Immediate permanent ban and referral to law enforcement.
